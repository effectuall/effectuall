    • Formulas in play for a hot-air balloon lift-off

        ➤ Buoyant Force is equal to the weight of air displaced by the hot-air balloon $$\begin{aligned} \text{Buoyant Force} &=\text{Pressure}\times \text{Area} \\ \text{B} &= (\rho_{\text{cold}}- \rho_{\text{hot}}) gh\times A \\ &= (\rho_{\text{cold}}- \rho_{\text{hot}}) gV \end{aligned}$$
        ➤Gas law equation $$\begin{aligned} PV &= nRT \\ \frac{n}{V} &= \frac{P}{RT} \\ \frac{\text{m}_{air}}{V} &= \frac{\text{M}_{air}P}{RT} \\ \frac{\text{m}_{air}}{V} &= \frac{\text{const}}{T}\\ \text{density, ρ} &= \frac{\text{const}}{T}\\ \text{ρ} &∝ \frac{1}{T} \\ \text{ (Density of air, ρ}& ⇒\text{ inversely proportional to temperature )}\\ \text{ρ}_{\text{hot}} &=\frac{\text{ρ}_{\text{cold}} T_{\text{cold}}}{T_{\text{hot}}}\\ &= \frac{\text{ρ}_{\text{c}} T_{\text{c}}}{T_{\text{h}}}\end{aligned}$$
    ➤ Buoyant Force $$\begin{aligned} \textbf{B} &= \Big(\rho_{\text{c}}- \frac{\rho_{\text{c}} T_{\text{c}}}{T_{\text{h}}}\Big) gV \\ \textbf{B} &= \rho_{\text{c}} \Big[ 1 - \frac{T_{\text{c}}}{T_{\text{h}}} \Big]gV\end{aligned}$$

    when Buoyant Force is greater than the Weight, the Hot-Air Balloon lifts. i.e

    $$\begin{aligned} \textbf{B} &= mg \\ \rho_{\text{c}} \Big[ 1 - \frac{T_{\text{c}}}{T_{\text{h}}} \Big]gV &= mg\\ or, \Big[ 1 - \frac{T_{\text{c}}}{T_{\text{h}}} \Big]&= \frac{m}{\rho_{\text{c}} V} \end{aligned}$$

    Question

    The mass of a hot-air balloon and its cargo (not including the air inside) is 200 kg. The air outside is at 10.0 °C and 101 kPa. The volume of the balloon is 400m3. To what temperature must the air in the balloon be heated before the balloon will lift off? (Air density at 10.0°C is 1.25 kg/m3.)

    Calculation:
    Given:- density, ρ = 1.25 kg/m3; mass, m = 200 kg; volume, V = 400 m3; Tc= 10+273 = 283K $$\begin{aligned} 1 - \frac{T_{\text{c}}}{T_{\text{h}}} &=\Big( \frac{200}{1.25 \times 400}\Big) \\ 1 - \frac{T_{\text{c}}}{T_{\text{h}}} &= \frac{1}{2.50} \\ 1 - \frac{283}{T_{\text{h}}} &= 0.4 \\ 0.6 &= \frac{283}{T_{\text{h}}} \\ T_{\text{h}} &= \frac{283}{0.6} \\ T_{\text{h}} &= 472K \\or, T_{\text{h}} &= 472 -273 = 199\text{°C}\end{aligned}$$

    Formulas used

    $$\begin{aligned} \text{Pressure, } P &= \frac{\text{Force}}{\text{Area}}\\ \text{Pressure, } P &= \text{ρgh} \\ \text{Density, } ρ &= \frac{\text{Mass}}{\text{Volumn}} \end{aligned}$$
    where $$\begin{aligned} \text{M}_{air} &=\text{Molecular Mass of air} \\ \text{m}_{air} &= \text{Given mass of air} \\ {{\text{m}_{air}}\over{\text{M}_{air}}} &= n \text{ , No. of moles }\end{aligned}$$
