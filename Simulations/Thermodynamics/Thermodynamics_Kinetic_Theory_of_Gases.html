    A cylinder contains a mixture of He and Ar gas in equilibrium at 150 °C. (a)What is the average kinetic energy (K.E) for each type of gas molecule? (b) What is the root-mean-square speed of each type of molecule.

    Calculation for the He and Ar gas (monoatomic gas) is shown below.

    Average Kinetic Energy Data
    Boltzmann Constant, kB = 1.38 x 10-23 J/K
    Atomic Mass of He, mHe =  g
    Atomic Mass of Ar, mAr =  g
    Temperature, T =  °C
    or, T =  K

    Enter a different of temperature to calculate the K.E

    \(\overline {K.E} \) = \( \frac {1}{2} mv_{rms}^{2}\) = \(\frac {3}{2} k_{B}T\)
    \(\overline {K.E} \) = \(\frac {3}{2} k_{B}T\)
    = \(\frac {3}{2}\)\(\times\) 1.38 \(\times 10^{-23}\times\) 423 \(\text{J}\)
    \(\overline {K.E} \) = 8.76 x 10-21

    Root Mean Square velocity, can be calculated from

    \(\overline {K.E} \) = \( \frac {1}{2} mv_{rms}^{2}\)
    \( \frac {1}{2} mv_{rms}^{2}\) = 8.76 x 10-21 J
    \( v_{rms}^{2}\) = \(\frac{2}{m} \times\) 8.76 x 10-21

    where \(m\) is the mass of the gas particles.
